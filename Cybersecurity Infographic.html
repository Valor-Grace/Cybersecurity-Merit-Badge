What is Cybersecurity?

It’s the practice of being a digital Scout: defending computers, networks, and data from malicious attacks. This infographic will guide you through the key themes of the merit badge, from fundamentals to future careers.

Your Digital Self: Safety & Ethics

Your first line of defense is you. Understanding your online presence and applying Scout values are the foundation of good cybersecurity.

Your Digital Footprint (Req. 1)

Every time you go online, you leave a trail. This "digital footprint" is composed of your activity across various platforms. Managing it is key to protecting your privacy.

Ethics in Cyberspace (Req. 2)

The Scout Law is your compass online. Being Trustworthy, Loyal, and Kind applies just as much to your digital actions as your physical ones.

A Scout is Trustworthy

Don't share misinformation or use others' accounts without permission.

A Scout is Kind

Stand up to cyberbullying and create a positive online environment.

A Scout is Obedient

Respect the terms of service for websites and apps you use.

The C.I.A. Triad (Req. 3)

These three principles are the bedrock of all cybersecurity. Every defense is built to protect at least one of them.

C

Confidentiality

Keeping secrets secret. Preventing unauthorized access to information.

I

Integrity

Ensuring data is accurate and trustworthy, not tampered with.

A

Availability

Making sure data and systems are accessible when needed.

The Expanding Cyber World

Cybersecurity extends beyond your computer to everyday objects and future careers.

The Internet of Things (IoT) (Req. 7)

More devices connect to the internet every day, from smart watches to home thermostats. Each is a potential entry point for attackers if not properly secured.

Careers in Cybersecurity (Req. 9)

This field is growing rapidly, with many exciting career paths for those with the right skills and a desire to protect others.

  • Security Analyst: The digital detective, monitoring networks for suspicious activity.
  • Ethical Hacker: Paid to legally break into systems to find weaknesses before criminals do.
  • Digital Forensics Expert: Recovers and investigates material found in digital devices.
  • Security Architect: Designs and builds secure computer systems from the ground up.
